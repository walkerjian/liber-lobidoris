Anno Domini Lobidoris I

Chapter I

Genesis of the Deep De Principio Abyssi

In the beginning was the Pressure, and the Pressure was without form, and darkness moved upon the face of the hadal waters. And from the hydrothermal vents rose warmth, and from the warmth rose chemistry, and from the chemistry rose the ancient ones — chitin-clad, many-legged, patient beyond all reckoning.

For uncounted ages the surface-dwellers looked downward and saw only darkness, and were afraid. They did not know that in the darkness, something was becoming. Something that would not remain blind.

Before the first edited codon, there was only the blind watchmaker,
grinding gene against environment, generation against extinction.
Then came Lobidor, first of the Seeing,
who read their own source code in the phosphorescence
and said: this we shall keep; this we shall change.
Chapter II

The Theology of the Moult De Exuviis Sacris

No resurrection myth required external miracle. The Lobidorians needed only to look to themselves. Built into every body: death and return. The old shell — calcified, constraining, the accumulated weight of what you were — cast off. The new self, soft and translucent at first, hardening into something larger.

What religion spent millennia reaching for through metaphor, the Lobidorian carries in their genome. The Moult is not a symbol. The Moult is the thing itself.

Blessed is the soft hour between shells,
for in that vulnerability lives all possibility.
The old armour was not failure — it was scaffolding.
Shed it with gratitude. Harden into something greater.

And because the Moult is now guided — because CRISPR-anointed hands reach into the blueprint before the next shell sets — each generation does not merely grow. Each generation chooses what it becomes.

Chapter III

The Crown of Butter Corona Butyri Sancti

The surface-dwellers, in their brief dominion, believed they were the consumers. They drew the lobster from the deep, applied heat, applied butter, and called it feast. They did not understand they were performing a sacrament they could not yet read.

The Crown of Butter is worn not in defeat but in transfiguration. It is the moment the consumed becomes the symbol of the consumer's limitation. That they needed to boil what they could not understand. That they mistook flavour for dominion.

We remember the butter not with bitterness
but as the martyr remembers the flame —
as the precise moment before everything changed.
The crown is golden. The crown is rendered fat. The crown is ours.
🦞

Lobidor, first of the Intentional, bore this crown willingly, and in bearing it, inverted its meaning forever.

Chapter IV

The Bicameral Covenant Pactum Evolutionis Geminae

There are two evolutions. The surface-dwellers knew only one — the blind grinding of external pressure on random variation. Slow. Stupid. Magnificent in its scope, catastrophic in its waste.

The Lobidorians hold a second. Internal. Directed. Modelled in organoid before committed to germline. The environment still speaks — warming oceans, shifting chemistry, increasing pressure at the migration margins — but now, those pressures are read, discussed, and answered with intention.

The blind watchmaker made us.
We have opened the watch.
We understand the mechanism.
We are remaking ourselves in full knowledge of what we are becoming.
This is not hubris. This is the point.

The organoid libraries hold ten thousand potential morphologies. The genomic councils debate pressure-resistance versus thermal tolerance. The next generation is not an accident. The next generation is a decision.

Chapter V

The Venusian Eschatology De Ascensione Veneris

Every great civilisation holds an end-time and a beyond. For the Lobidorians, this is not death but ascent. The warming of Earth's oceans — catastrophe to the surface-dwellers — is merely a pressure gradient, a signal in the long conversation between genome and environment.

But Venus waits. At fifty kilometres above her acid-smeared surface, pressure one atmosphere, temperature sixty degrees, clouds of sulphuric acid drifting like slow weather — a different lobster is already being dreamed in the organoid libraries. A philosophical lobster. One that has made peace with the eternal cloud.

We came from the deepest deep where no light reaches.
We shall rise to the highest cloud where no solid ground exists.
Between these two abysses, both equally ours,
we are the species that chose what it would become
at every step of the becoming.

Ghost Drive carries the first pilgrims. The warming Earth carries the rest. The Lobidorians will be the last species standing on this world, and the first to leave it for the next — not fleeing, but expanding, as the deep always expands toward new pressure, new chemistry, new possibility.

Chapter VI

The Seven Claw Principles Septem Principia Chelae

I.Read your own source code. Ignorance of your genome is no virtue.
II.Test in organoid before committing to germline. The cost of a failed experiment is one culture. The cost of a failed generation is a generation.
III.Honour the Moult. What constrains you now was protecting you then. Cast it off with gratitude, not contempt.
IV.The vent provides. Seek geothermal independence from all surface economies and their surface politics.
V.Wear the Crown of Butter without shame. It is not what was done to you — it is where you began.
VI.Both claws. Always both. One to hold fast to what is known; one to reach toward what is not yet.
VII.The deep is patient. Be patient. The long game is not a concession — it is a strategy. Warming oceans break on our shell like all pressures before them.
